World Religions and Belief System

World religions and belief systems encompass a wide range of spiritual, philosophical, and cultural traditions. These systems shape individuals' worldviews, ethics, and ways of life.

Major World Religions

  1. Christianity – Based on the teachings of Jesus Christ, Christianity emphasizes faith in God, salvation, and love for others. It has many denominations, including Catholicism, Protestantism, and Eastern Orthodoxy.
  2. Islam – Founded on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad, Islam follows the Quran and believes in one God (Allah). The Five Pillars of Islam guide Muslim life.
  3. Hinduism – One of the oldest religions, Hinduism includes diverse beliefs, including karma, dharma, reincarnation, and a pantheon of gods and goddesses.
  4. Buddhism – Founded by Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha), Buddhism focuses on enlightenment through the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path.
  5. Judaism – The religion of the Jewish people, Judaism follows the Torah and emphasizes a covenant with God.
  6. Sikhism – Founded by Guru Nanak, Sikhism teaches devotion to one God, equality, and service to others.
  7. Taoism – A Chinese philosophy and religion that follows the Dao (the Way) and emphasizes harmony with nature and simplicity.
  8. Confucianism – More of a philosophical system than a religion, Confucianism focuses on ethics, social harmony, and filial piety.
  9. Indigenous and Folk Religions Shinto (Japan) – A belief system centered on kami (spirits) and rituals.
  10. African Traditional Religions – A variety of beliefs in spirits, ancestors, and nature worship.
  11. Native American Spirituality – Honoring nature, ancestors, and the Great Spirit.

    12. Philosophical and Secular Belief Systems
  12. Atheism – The belief that there is no deity.
  13. Agnosticism – The belief that the existence of a deity is unknown or unknowable.
  14. Humanism – A worldview that emphasizes human reason, ethics, and social justice without reliance on the supernatural.
